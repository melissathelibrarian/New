The Los Angeles Public Library serves one of the largest communities in the United States, with a population of over 10 million people. The library itself serves over 3 million citizens in this diverse, multicultural city; circulation nears 10 million on an annual basis. It can be quite the task serving so many patrons from such diverse backgrounds, however, Los Angeles County Public Library proves itself to be the backbone of the community, time and again by providing innovative services, programming, and resources for this unique community.

However, serving a community of such a large population and diverse backgrounds certainly comes with its share of challenges.

As we know, representation matters and LA County Library must work hard to ensure adequate representation and materials to meet a wide range of patron needs. From the data, we know that in order to best serve the community the library will need to:

  1. Provide outreach to the communities they are not reaching with traditional services
  2. Ensure that staff people speak multiple languages
  3. Create programming that meets the needs of underserved communities
  4. Partner with local organizations to reach patrons experiencing homelessness
  5. Offer a wide-range of materials, in various languages and formats

Strategic Initiatives:

  • Tell the Library Story - Improve the way we tell our customers about the wide range of services and programs available at local community libraries.
  • Affirm the Library as a Center for Learning - Address our communities’ learning and literacy needs through classes, events and online technology.
  • Expand and Support the Digital Library - Add more digital content and make it easier to find and use. Expand mobile access.
  • Transform the Role of the Library as Place - Increase the role of the library in the community and offer services beyond the library’s walls. Customize each library to the community it serves.
  • Support and Cultivate the Community’s Creativity - Introduce collaborative work spaces to learn new tools and technologies or share a creative hobby or interest.
  • Develop the Library as a Center for Community Engagement - Collaborate with the community’s cultural groups and broaden our leadership role as a trusted source for equal access to information.
  • Develop a Staff Prepared for the Future - Improve your library experience by developing our staff’s leadership and technology skills.
